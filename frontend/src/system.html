The Scoring System

Solving the national over-storming problem

Overview

Stormalytics evaluates a field or court storming along three dimensions. Upset: how unexpected was the win? Impact: what was the meaning of the win? Excitement: how exciting was game, particularly the ending?

Each category gets a score, and if the total score is high enough then the storm is acceptable. Otherwise, the fans need to get back in their seats.

Storming Threshold

A total score of 2.5 or higher indicates that a field or court storming is justified.

Each category is scored from 0.0 to 1.0, with a 0.5 bonus available for exceptional circumstances.

Degree of Upset

The degree to which the result defies expectations based on rankings, records, and conventional wisdom.

0.0

Completely expected result. Good job, your team won a game.

0.5

The underdog won, but you're only a little surprised.

1.0

A season-defining upset. You are shocked.

1.5

Historic upset. These are the ones that average fans can recall years later.

Game Impact

The significance of this game's meaning to season direction, rivalries, and recent matchup history.

0.0

Just an average win.

0.5

A rivalry, some postseason impact, satisfies karma,relieves recent pain.

1.0

Big rivalry, major postseason impact, rights an historical wrong.

1.5

Defines a program or an era.

Game Excitement

The game's intensity, drama, memorable momentsand back-and-forth - especially at the end of the game.

0.0

The only drama is the storming.

0.5

An exciting game: close throughout and not decided until late.

1.0

Decided on the final play of the game.

1.5

Decided on the final play of the game in an extraordinary way.
